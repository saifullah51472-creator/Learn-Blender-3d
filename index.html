Before starting with Blender, make sure your system meets the following specifications for smooth performance. Here are the three main categories based on usage and performance level:

üü¢ Compatible Systems (Minimum Requirements)

If you are a beginner or just starting your journey with Blender, these are the minimum system requirements that will allow you to install and run the software for learning and creating small projects. These specifications are suitable for simple modeling, low-resolution rendering, and basic animation.

  • CPU: Dual-core 64-bit processor (Intel or AMD)
  • RAM: 8 GB
  • GPU: Graphics card with 2 GB VRAM and support for OpenGL 4.3+
  • Storage: At least 3 GB of free disk space
  • Operating System: Windows 10, macOS 10.13, or Linux

üü° Recommended Systems

For smoother performance and faster rendering, these specifications are recommended. They provide a balanced setup for most users who want to work on moderate 3D scenes, animations, and detailed projects efficiently.

  • CPU: Quad-core processor (Intel i5 / AMD Ryzen 5 or newer)
  • RAM: 16 GB
  • GPU: NVIDIA GTX 1660 / AMD RX 580 (4 GB VRAM or more)
  • Storage: SSD with 10 GB free for project files
  • Display: Full HD (1920x1080) resolution or higher
  • Operating System: Windows 10/11, macOS 12+, or Linux

üî¥ Professional / Ultimate Setup

For professionals working on high-resolution renders, VFX, or large-scale projects, these specifications deliver exceptional performance and stability. This setup is ideal for studios or advanced artists demanding top-tier rendering speeds and multitasking.

  • CPU: 8-core or higher (Intel i9 / Ryzen 9 / Apple M3 Pro or newer)
  • RAM: 32 GB or more
  • GPU: NVIDIA RTX 4070 / AMD Radeon Pro / Apple M3 GPU (8 GB+ VRAM)
  • Storage: NVMe SSD with 50 GB free for cache and large projects
  • Display: 4K UHD or Ultrawide monitor (color-calibrated)
  • Extras: Pen tablet, dual monitors, and cooling setup
